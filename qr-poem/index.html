Ranzyl Mark Anoche

The Weight of Questions

Philosophy is not loud.
It does not shout like the world does.

It whispers at 2 a.m.,
when the lights are off
and your thoughts are finally brave enough
to speak.

It asks,
Who are you
when no one is watching?

What is love
when it is not returned?

What is success
when the applause fades?

Philosophy sits beside you
when you stare at the ceiling,
when you wonder why certain things
make your heart feel full —
and others leave it echoing.

It is the space
between a question
and the courage to face its answer.

It does not promise comfort.
It promises clarity.

And sometimes,
clarity
is heavier than confusion.
